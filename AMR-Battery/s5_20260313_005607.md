As a battery engineer specializing in outdoor Autonomous Mobile Robots (AMRs), I have analyzed the requirements for last-mile delivery and sidewalk robots. These platforms operate in "unstructured" environments, requiring a balance between high energy density for range and ruggedness for environmental exposure.

### 2. Battery Sizing & Selection Logic

#### The Range/Weight Calculation:
For a 60kg robot traveling at 5-8 km/h on sidewalks:
*   **Average consumption:** ~50 Wh/km (accounts for rolling resistance and sensor/compute power).
*   **Required Range:** 40km.
*   **Required Energy:** $40km \times 50 Wh/km = 2000 Wh (2kWh)$.
*   **Weight Impact:** Using NMC (~160 Wh/kg pack level), this pack weighs **12.5kg**. Using LFP (~100 Wh/kg pack level), the weight jumps to **20kg**, significantly impacting the robot's ability to climb curbs and increasing motor strain.

#### Why NMC is preferred over LFP in this segment:
While LFP (Lithium Iron Phosphate) offers superior safety and cycle life (>2000 cycles), **NMC is the industry standard for sidewalk AMRs** for three reasons:
1.  **Curb Climbing:** Every kilogram added to the battery requires larger motors and sturdier suspension to jump curbs. NMC's 40-50% weight advantage is decisive for "sidewalk agility."
2.  **Cold Weather Performance:** NMC generally maintains higher voltage and capacity at -20°C compared to LFP, which suffers from high internal resistance in sub-zero winters.
3.  **Volume:** LFP takes up nearly 2x the volume for the same energy, leaving less room for the "Last-Mile" delivery parcel.

### 3. Key Trade-offs

*   **Safety vs. Range:** NMC has a higher risk of thermal runaway (210°C) compared to LFP (270°C+). This is mitigated by using **Phase Change Materials (PCM)** or flame-retardant potting inside the pack to prevent cell-to-cell propagation.
*   **Fast Charging vs. Cycle Life:** 1C charging allows for quick turnarounds but degrades the battery faster. Most fleets prefer **Swappable Packs** to allow slow, healthy charging (0.5C) off-robot.
*   **Cost vs. Durability:** Automotive-grade 21700 cells are expensive but necessary for the 1000+ vibration cycles an outdoor robot faces.

### 4. Failure Modes in Outdoor AMR Environments

1.  **Moisture Ingress/Corrosion:** Micro-cracks in seals due to thermal cycling (winter/summer) allow humidity to enter, causing BMS sensor drift or short circuits.
2.  **Spot-Weld Fatigue:** Constant vibration from cobblestones and curbs can snap nickel tab welds, leading to "lost" parallel groups and sudden voltage drops.
3.  **Dendrite Growth (Cold Charging):** If the BMS fails to engage heaters and charges at sub-zero temps, lithium dendrites form, leading to internal shorts and potential fire.
