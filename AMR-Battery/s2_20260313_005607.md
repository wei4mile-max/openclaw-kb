| Feature | Lead-Acid (Traction) | Lithium-Ion (LiFePO4) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Energy Density** | 30–40 Wh/kg | 90–120 Wh/kg |
| **Charging Time** | 8–12 hours (needs "cool down") | 1–2 hours (Fast Charge) |
| **Maintenance** | High (Watering, Equalization) | Zero (BMS Managed) |

| **Counterweight** | Naturally heavy (Lead is dense) | Lighter; requires integrated steel ballast plates. |

### Key Trade-offs

1.  **Energy Density vs. Ballast Requirements:** While Lithium is more energy-dense, forklifts require weight for stability. Transitioning to Lithium often requires adding "dead weight" (steel plates) to the battery box to maintain the forklift's lifting capacity.
2.  **Fast Swap vs. Opportunity Charging:** 
    *   *Fast Swap (IEC 62840):* Best for 24/7 operations where grid capacity is limited. Requires expensive robotic swap stations.
    *   *Opportunity Charging:* Preferred for AMRs. Charging for 15 mins every 2 hours at "stations" eliminates the need for battery swapping but requires higher C-rate cells.
3.  **Voltage Choice:** Higher voltage (80V) increases efficiency but increases the cost of semiconductors in the BMS and motor controllers compared to standard 48V systems.

### Failure Modes in Heavy-Duty AMR Applications

1.  **Vibration-Induced Interconnect Failure:** Heavy-duty AMRs operating on uneven warehouse floors experience constant micro-vibrations, leading to fatigue in busbar welds.
2.  **BMS "Nuisance Tripping":** High inrush currents from hydraulic pumps can cause the BMS to shut down if the over-current protection (OCP) logic isn't tuned for inductive loads.
3.  **Deep Discharge Damage:** If an autonomous unit loses its path or fails to dock, it can discharge to 0% SOC. Without a "Deep Sleep" mode, the parasitic draw from the BMS can kill the cells.
4.  **Thermal Stratification:** In large 800Ah packs, center cells can become 10–15°C hotter than exterior cells, leading to uneven aging and pack imbalance.
