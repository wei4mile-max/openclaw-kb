### Why Opportunity Charging (2C-3C) is the Defining Spec
In modern high-velocity warehouses, **uptime is the only metric that matters.** 
*   **Elimination of Swapping:** Manual battery swapping requires dedicated staff, charging rooms, and 2 batteries per robot.
*   **24/7 Availability:** By charging at **2C-3C**, an AMR can recover roughly 25-30% of its state-of-charge (SOC) in just 10 minutes. 
*   **Infrastructure Efficiency:** Fewer chargers are needed if robots can "sip" power rapidly during short idle windows rather than "gulping" power for 4-8 hours at a stationary dock.

2.  **Energy Density vs. Safety:** NMC offers higher energy density (longer runtime in same space), but its lower thermal runaway temperature makes it a liability in high-density automated warehouses compared to LFP.
3.  **Weight vs. Traction:** Unlike EVs, AMRs sometimes *need* weight for traction when carrying heavy shelves. Replacing heavy lead-acid with light Lithium often requires adding "dead weight" (ballast) to the chassis.

4.  **Inaccurate SOC Drift:** Constant shallow opportunity charging (e.g., staying between 40% and 80%) prevents the BMS from seeing "full" or "empty" states, causing the SOC estimate to drift over time.

1.  **Current Density:** A 70-tonne AGV accelerating needs ~300kW of peak power. At 80V, this is **3,750 Amps**, requiring busbars and cables the size of a human arm. At 700V, this is only **~430 Amps**, which is manageable with standard automotive-grade cabling.
2.  **Efficiency:** High-voltage systems significantly reduce heat loss ($P_{loss} = I^2R$). Low-voltage systems would waste 10–15% of energy just in cable heat during peak loads.
3.  **Charging Speed:** Standard forklift batteries (Lead-Acid or basic Li-ion) cannot dissipate heat fast enough for 300kW+ fast charging. They lack the active liquid cooling integration required for the 24/7 "Charge-and-Go" cycle.

---

### Key Trade-offs
*   **Energy Density vs. Life (LFP vs. LTO):** LFP (Lithium Iron Phosphate) offers better cost and density for large 400kWh packs. LTO (Lithium Titanate) offers 20,000+ cycles and 6C charging but at 3x the cost and 50% the energy density.
*   **Active Cooling Complexity:** Liquid cooling adds pumps, radiators, and leak-risk but is the only way to enable high-current fast charging in ports.
*   **Modular Weight vs. Volume:** Drawer-style modularity increases the total footprint due to structural frames and connectors but reduces MTTR (Mean Time To Repair).

### Failure Modes in Heavy AMR Environments
1.  **Thermal Gradient Stress:** In large packs, the "middle" cells often run 10°C hotter than the edges, leading to premature aging of the center modules if the liquid cooling manifold is poorly designed.
2.  **Vibration-Induced Interconnect Fatigue:** High-current busbar welds can crack under the constant vibration of 70-tonne loads on uneven pier surfaces, leading to localized arcing.
3.  **Galvanic Corrosion:** In port environments, if the IP67 seal is breached, salt-laden air creates a conductive path between high-voltage terminals and the chassis (Isolation Fault).
